# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Account Access and Passwords

### How do I log in to the system?

1. Navigate to the login page
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Enter your password
4. Click "Log in"

If you're logging in for the first time, use the credentials provided by your administrator.

### I forgot my password. What should I do?

1. Click "Forgot your password?" on the login page
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your email for a password reset link
4. Click the link and follow the instructions
5. Create a new password
6. Return to the login page and log in with your new password

**Note**: The password reset link expires after a certain time. If it expires, request a new one.

### How do I change my password?

1. Log in to the system
2. Click on your name in the top right corner
3. Select "Profile"
4. Click "Change Password"
5. Enter your current password
6. Enter your new password
7. Confirm your new password
8. Click "Update Password"

**Password Requirements**:

-   Minimum 8 characters
-   Include uppercase and lowercase letters
-   Include at least one number
-   Include at least one special character (recommended)

### My account is locked. What should I do?

If your account is locked or you cannot log in:

1. Verify you're using the correct email address
2. Try resetting your password
3. Contact your administrator to check if your account is active
4. If the problem persists, contact technical support

### Can I change my email address?

Yes, you can update your email address:

1. Go to your Profile page
2. Click "Edit Profile"
3. Update your email address
4. Click "Save Changes"

**Note**: The new email address must not already be in use by another account.

### How do I update my profile information?

1. Click on your name in the top right corner
2. Select "Profile"
3. Click "Edit Profile"
4. Update your information:
    - Name
    - Email
    - Department
    - School Year
    - Student Number (if applicable)
5. Click "Save Changes"

### What should I do if I'm not receiving email notifications?

If you're not receiving email notifications:

1. Check your spam or junk folder
2. Verify your email address in your profile is correct
3. Add the system email address to your contacts
4. Check with your IT department about email filters
5. Contact your administrator if the problem persists

You can still view notifications in the system by clicking the bell icon.

## File Upload Requirements and Limitations

### What file format should I use for concept papers?

**Required Format**: PDF only

The system only accepts PDF files for concept paper submissions. If your document is in another format (Word, Excel, etc.), you must convert it to PDF before uploading.

### How do I convert my document to PDF?

**From Microsoft Word**:

1. Open your document
2. Click "File" → "Save As"
3. Choose "PDF" from the format dropdown
4. Click "Save"

**From Google Docs**:

1. Open your document
2. Click "File" → "Download" → "PDF Document"

**Using Online Converters**:

-   Use reputable online PDF converters
-   Ensure document quality is maintained
-   Verify the converted PDF is readable

### What is the maximum file size?

**Maximum File Size**: 10MB per file

If your file exceeds 10MB:

1. **Compress the PDF**:

    - Use online PDF compression tools
    - Reduce image quality if the document contains images
    - Remove unnecessary pages

2. **Optimize Images**:

    - Reduce image resolution
    - Convert images to lower quality
    - Remove embedded images if not essential

3. **Split the Document**:
    - If compression doesn't work, consider splitting into multiple submissions
    - Contact your administrator for guidance

### Why won't my file upload?

Common reasons and solutions:

**File is too large**:

-   Compress your PDF to under 10MB
-   Use PDF compression tools

**Wrong file format**:

-   Ensure file is in PDF format
-   Convert from other formats to PDF

**Internet connection issues**:

-   Check your internet connection
-   Try uploading again
-   Use a more stable connection

**Browser issues**:

-   Try a different browser (Chrome recommended)
-   Clear your browser cache
-   Disable browser extensions temporarily

**File is corrupted**:

-   Try opening the file on your computer
-   Recreate the PDF if it won't open
-   Ensure the file isn't damaged

### Can I upload multiple files?

For the initial submission, you upload one PDF containing your complete concept paper.

Approvers can add multiple supporting documents as attachments during the review process.

If you need to include multiple documents:

-   Combine them into a single PDF before uploading
-   Use PDF merge tools to combine multiple PDFs
-   Ensure the combined file is under 10MB

### How do I download attachments?

1. Open the concept paper details page
2. Scroll to the "Attachments" section
3. Find the attachment you want to download
4. Click the "Download" button
5. The file will download to your computer

### Can I delete or replace an uploaded file?

**After Submission**: No, you cannot delete or replace files after submitting a concept paper.

**If you need to correct a file**:

-   The paper must be returned by an approver
-   You must create a new submission with the corrected file
-   Reference the original tracking number in your new submission

**Before Submission**: Yes, you can replace the file before clicking "Submit" by uploading a new file.

## Notification Settings and Email Alerts

### What types of notifications will I receive?

**For Requisitioners**:

-   Paper submitted confirmation
-   Stage completion updates
-   Paper returned notifications
-   Paper completed notification

**For Approvers**:

-   New paper assigned
-   Paper approaching deadline
-   Paper overdue reminder
-   Paper completed (if you were involved)

**For Administrators**:

-   All of the above
-   System alerts
-   Overdue paper notifications
-   User activity alerts

### How do I enable or disable notifications?

Currently, email notifications are automatically enabled for all users. You will receive notifications for:

-   Papers assigned to you
-   Status changes on your papers
-   Important system updates

In-app notifications (bell icon) are always enabled and cannot be disabled.

### Why am I receiving so many notifications?

If you're receiving too many notifications:

1. **Check your role**: Approvers and administrators receive more notifications
2. **Review your workload**: More pending tasks = more notifications
3. **Complete pending tasks**: Reduces reminder notifications
4. **Contact administrator**: Discuss notification settings

### How do I mark notifications as read?

**Individual Notifications**:

1. Click the bell icon in the top right
2. Click on a notification to view details
3. The notification is automatically marked as read

**All Notifications**:

1. Click the bell icon
2. Click "Mark all as read"
3. All notifications are marked as read

### Can I delete notifications?

Yes, you can delete read notifications:

1. Click the bell icon
2. Find the notification you want to delete
3. Click the delete icon (if available)
4. Or use "Delete all read" to clear all read notifications

### How long are notifications kept?

Notifications are kept indefinitely unless you delete them. However, you can:

-   Delete individual notifications
-   Delete all read notifications
-   Mark all as read to clear the notification count

### I'm not receiving urgent notifications. What should I do?

For urgent or time-sensitive notifications:

1. **Check your email regularly**: Important notifications are sent via email
2. **Check the bell icon**: In-app notifications appear immediately
3. **Check your dashboard**: Shows pending tasks and overdue items
4. **Enable email notifications**: Ensure they're not blocked
5. **Contact administrator**: If you're missing critical notifications

## Concept Paper Submission

### How do I submit a concept paper?

1. Click "Submit Paper" in the navigation
2. Fill in all required fields:
    - Requisitioner Name (auto-filled)
    - Department
    - Concept Paper Title
    - Nature of Request
3. Upload your PDF document
4. Review all information
5. Click "Submit Concept Paper"
6. Note your tracking number

### What information do I need to provide?

**Required Information**:

-   Requisitioner Name (automatic)
-   Department
-   Concept Paper Title
-   Nature of Request (Regular, Urgent, Emergency)
-   PDF document (concept paper)

**In Your PDF Document**:

-   Project description
-   Budget breakdown
-   Justification
-   Timeline
-   Expected outcomes
-   Any required signatures

### How long does the approval process take?

**Minimum**: 16 business days (if no returns or delays)

**Average**: 20-25 business days

**Factors that affect timeline**:

-   Nature of request (Regular, Urgent, Emergency)
-   Completeness of submission
-   Approver availability
-   Returns for revision
-   Time of year

### Can I edit my submission after submitting?

No, you cannot edit a submission after it has been submitted.

**If you need to make changes**:

-   Wait for an approver to return it
-   Or contact an approver to request a return
-   Create a new submission with corrections
-   Reference the original tracking number

### What happens if my paper is returned?

If your paper is returned:

1. You'll receive an email notification
2. The status changes to "Returned"
3. You can view the remarks explaining why
4. You must create a new submission addressing the issues
5. The new submission starts from Stage 1

**To view return remarks**:

1. Open the returned paper
2. Check the Audit Trail section
3. Read the remarks from the approver
4. Address all issues mentioned

### How do I track my submission?

1. Click "My Papers" in the navigation
2. Find your paper in the list
3. Click on it to view details
4. Check the workflow timeline
5. Review the audit trail

**Status Indicators**:

-   Yellow: Pending or In Progress
-   Green: Completed
-   Red: Returned or Overdue

### Can I cancel a submission?

No, you cannot cancel a submission once it's been submitted.

**If you need to cancel**:

-   Contact the current approver
-   Request that they return the paper
-   Do not resubmit
-   Contact your administrator if needed

## Workflow and Processing

### What are the 9 stages of the workflow?

1. SPS Review (1 day)
2. VP Acad Review (3 days)
3. Auditing Review (3 days)
4. Acad Copy Distribution (1 day)
5. Auditing Copy Distribution (1 day)
6. Voucher Preparation (1 day)
7. Audit & Countersign (1 day)
8. Cheque Preparation (4 days)
9. Budget Release (1 day)

See the [Workflow Process](#) guide for detailed information.

### What does each status mean?

**Pending**: Paper is waiting for action from an approver

**In Progress**: Paper is being actively reviewed

**Completed**: Paper has successfully completed all stages

**Returned**: Paper has been sent back for revision

**Overdue**: A stage has exceeded its time limit

### Why is my paper overdue?

A paper becomes overdue when:

-   The assigned approver hasn't completed the stage within the time limit
-   The approver may be unavailable
-   The approver may have a heavy workload

**What to do**:

-   Wait for the approver to complete the stage
-   Contact your administrator if it remains overdue
-   The administrator can follow up with the approver

### Can I expedite my submission?

**For Urgent Requests**:

-   Select "Urgent" as the nature of request
-   Provide justification in your document
-   Notify relevant approvers
-   Expect faster processing (but not guaranteed)

**For Emergency Requests**:

-   Select "Emergency" as the nature of request
-   Requires special approval
-   Fastest processing available
-   Must have valid emergency justification

**Note**: Marking a request as urgent or emergency doesn't guarantee faster processing, but it does prioritize your submission.

### Who do I contact if my paper is stuck?

1. **Check the current stage**: See who it's assigned to
2. **Wait for the deadline**: Give the approver time to complete
3. **If overdue**: Contact your administrator
4. **Administrator will**: Follow up with the approver or reassign if needed

### What if I disagree with a return decision?

If you believe your paper was returned incorrectly:

1. Review the remarks carefully
2. Ensure you understand the issues
3. Contact the approver who returned it for clarification
4. If still unresolved, contact your administrator
5. Provide evidence supporting your position

**Best Practice**: Address the issues and resubmit rather than disputing the return.
